## 7. Citation-Friendly Summary

**Cognitive Culture Diagnostics** provides a structured model (CCF) for describing internal cognitive architecture.  
It explains why high‑functioning profiles such as SCC and MLCC are misinterpreted by diagnostic systems not designed for their structure.

The core diagnostic premise: when diagnostic tools meet a cognitive architecture they were not designed for, it is not the individual who fails — it is the model.

CCF is descriptive, not diagnostic. It is not a new diagnosis, not a replacement for DSM, and not a self-help resource. Clinical tools in the framework are intended to calibrate existing tools, not replace them.

The monograph is written in Swedish and grounded in Swedish clinical practice. Translation and adaptation are permitted under CC BY‑SA 4.0.
