# Meta Documentation

This directory contains **project-level documentation** for the Cognitive Culture Diagnostics monograph.

The files in `meta/` do not contain theoretical arguments, clinical claims, or case material.  
They exist to document **structure, scope, terminology, methodology, and design decisions** that govern the project as a whole.

This layer is intended for:
- future editors or collaborators,
- academic reviewers,
- and the author’s own long-term orientation.

---

## Purpose of the Meta Layer

The meta layer serves three functions:

1. **Architectural stability**  
   To record why the project is structured the way it is, and which decisions are considered fixed.

2. **Epistemological clarity**  
   To make explicit what the project does and does not claim, and how key concepts are used.

3. **Methodological transparency**  
   To document design choices that affect interpretation, validity, and scope.

Nothing in `meta/` is meant to be read as part of the monograph itself.

---

## Files in This Directory

The following meta documents are planned or in use:

- `structure.md`  
  Describes the overall structure of the monograph (parts, chapters, appendices) and the rationale behind it.

- `scope-and-non-goals.md`  
  Defines what the project explicitly does **not** attempt to do, to prevent category drift or overextension.

- `terminology-policy.md`  
  Documents how key terms are used, redefined, or deliberately avoided (e.g. “rigidity”, “masking”, “culture”).

- `methodology-notes.md`  
  Meta-level notes on Self-as-Instrument Design (SAID), single-subject logic, introspective data, and falsifiability.

- `versioning-and-status.md`  
  Tracks the project’s development state and what constitutes a structural vs. content-level change.

- `publication-notes.md`  
  Notes related to dissemination, citation, licensing, and future publication formats.

Not all files need to be populated immediately.  
Placeholders are intentional and part of the design.

---

## Design Principles

- The meta layer is **descriptive, not persuasive**.
- It documents decisions; it does not argue for them.
- It prioritizes long-term clarity over short-term convenience.
- It exists to reduce future ambiguity, not to resolve current uncertainty.

---

## Relationship to the Main Text

- The monograph content lives in `content/sv/`.
- Clinical tools and reference material live in `content/sv/appendices/`.
- The meta layer governs both, but is not part of either.

If a question arises about *why* something is structured or framed in a certain way,  
the answer should be findable here.

---

## Status

The meta layer is considered **structurally stable** once created.  
Updates should be additive and explicitly documented.
