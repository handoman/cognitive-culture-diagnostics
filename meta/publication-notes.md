# Publication Notes

This document records considerations related to **publication, citation, and dissemination** of the Cognitive Culture Diagnostics project.

It does not define final publication formats, but establishes guiding principles.

---

## Intended Publication Forms

The project is designed to be publishable in multiple formats, including:

- Digital monograph (repository-based)
- PDF or print-ready document
- Academic preprint
- Educational or clinical reference material

The structure supports both linear reading and selective reference use.

---

## Citation Guidance

Until a formal publication venue is established, the project should be cited as:

> Christian Sandberg. *Cognitive Culture Diagnostics: Cognitive Architecture as a Missing Diagnostic Dimension*.  
> Unpublished monograph, Visby.

Once a stable public release (v1.0) is available, a canonical citation format should be defined here.

---

## Language and Accessibility

- The core monograph is written in Swedish.
- Project-level documentation is provided in English.
- This language split is intentional and reflects the clinical context of the work.

Future translations may be produced, but are not required for validity.

---

## Licensing Considerations

The project is intended to be:

- openly readable,
- citable with attribution,
- reusable for educational and clinical purposes.

Final license selection should balance:
- academic openness,
- protection against misrepresentation,
- and ethical use of clinical material.

License details should be added here once finalized.

---

## Use of Clinical Tools and Appendices

Appendices are designed for practical use.

They may be:
- printed,
- adapted for training,
- or integrated into clinical workflows,

provided that:
- attribution is maintained,
- the framework is not presented as a diagnostic replacement,
- and tools are not detached from their interpretive context.

---

## Future Dissemination

Potential future dissemination paths include:
- academic journals (theoretical or methodological focus),
- clinical education contexts,
- interdisciplinary research forums.

Any derivative publications should reference the monograph as the primary source.

---

## Stability Statement

These publication notes are **provisional**.

They may be updated as:
- publication venues are selected,
- licensing decisions are finalized,
- or dissemination strategies evolve.

Such updates do not affect the scientific or clinical content of the project.
