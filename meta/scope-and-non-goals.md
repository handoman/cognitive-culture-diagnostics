# Scope and Non-Goals

This document defines the **intended scope** of the Cognitive Culture Diagnostics project and explicitly states what the project does **not** attempt to do.

Its purpose is to prevent category drift, overextension, and misinterpretation of claims.

---

## Intended Scope

The project is designed to:

- Describe **cognitive architecture** as a diagnostic-relevant dimension.
- Introduce **Cognitive Culture Framework (CCF)** as a descriptive model.
- Analyze how certain cognitive cultures (SCC, MLCC) interact with existing diagnostic tools.
- Demonstrate these interactions through a transparent single-subject case.
- Translate findings into **clinical heuristics**, not diagnostic rules.
- Propose directions for future research and diagnostic system design.

The scope is intentionally **process-oriented**, not outcome-oriented.

---

## Explicit Non-Goals

The project does **not** aim to:

### 1. Introduce New Diagnoses

- SCC and MLCC are **not diagnostic categories**.
- They are not proposed as additions to DSM or ICD.
- They do not replace existing diagnostic constructs.

Cognitive culture is a **descriptive axis**, not a classificatory one.

---

### 2. Replace Existing Diagnostic Systems

- The project does not seek to replace DSM, ICD, or established instruments.
- It does not argue that current systems are invalid.
- It does not propose abandoning standardized assessment.

The framework is intended as a **complementary interpretive layer**, not an alternative system.

---

### 3. Generalize from a Single Case

- The single-subject analysis is not presented as representative.
- No population-level prevalence claims are made.
- No statistical generalization is implied.

The case functions as a **diagnostic stress test**, not as evidence of frequency.

---

### 4. Provide Treatment or Medication Guidelines

- The project does not prescribe treatment protocols.
- Medication discussions are interpretive, not prescriptive.
- Clinical recommendations focus on **assessment and interpretation**, not intervention.

Any treatment decisions remain the responsibility of licensed clinicians.

---

### 5. Pathologize Cognitive Styles

- Systemic or meta-logical cognition is not framed as pathology.
- Differences in representational mode are not deficits by default.
- The framework does not rank cognitive cultures hierarchically.

Variation is treated as **architectural diversity**, not deviation.

---

### 6. Function as a Self-Help or Identity Framework

- The project is not intended for self-diagnosis.
- It does not provide identity labels for personal use.
- It does not aim to create new neurodiversity categories.

Its primary audience is **clinical and research professionals**.

---

## Boundary Statement

All claims in the project should be interpreted within these boundaries.

If a conclusion appears to exceed this scope, it should be treated as:
- a hypothesis,
- a heuristic,
- or a prompt for further investigation,

not as a definitive claim.

---

## Stability Statement

The scope and non-goals defined here are considered **fixed**.

Future expansions of the project should:
- remain compatible with these boundaries,
- or explicitly document any scope changes in `versioning-and-status.md`.
